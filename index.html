What Makes Expansion Work
01
AE stays on the account — always

Never handed to CS. Day 1 post-signing, set the bi-weekly cadence. Months 1–3 are the highest-churn risk — be present, remove friction, capture ROI data.

02
The close is the start

Phase 1 signed = the real sales cycle begins. Tag expansion opportunities in CRM before the ink is dry. Review them at the first QBR.

03
Every QBR is a sales meeting

ROI realized → surface new bottleneck → introduce the next module as the natural next step. Never pitch cold — always anchor to their data.

04
Sell on their needs, not your roadmap

Only pitch a new module when their pain justifies it. Expansion seeds are planted in discovery — harvested when they're ready.

05
Make Shilpan a hero

Help him present results to Mary Barra. His career trajectory is your expansion map — his wins = your renewals and upsells.

06
New stakeholders = new budget

Map the org proactively — not reactively at renewal. Geographic expansion starts with one internal champion in the next division.
